Questions, answered

Why won't my iPhone photos open on Windows or Android?

iPhones save photos in Apple's HEIC format to save space. Many Windows PCs, Android phones, websites, and email clients can't read HEIC. Converting to JPG makes them open everywhere.

How do I convert HEIC to JPG on my iPhone?

Open the app, tap Select Photos, choose the photos or a whole album, pick JPG, and tap Convert. Then save to Photos, save to Files, or share. The whole thing takes a few seconds.

Are my photos uploaded anywhere?

Never. Every conversion happens entirely on your device using Apple's built-in frameworks. There are no servers, no accounts, and no analytics — your photos never leave your phone.

Is it really no subscription?

Yes. One payment of $4.99 unlocks unlimited conversions forever, including all future updates. No weekly or monthly fees, and no ads.

Can I convert in batches or to PNG and PDF?

Yes. Convert as many photos at once as you like. Output to JPG (opens everywhere), PNG (keeps transparency), or PDF (combine several photos into one document).

Does converting lower the quality?

No — it converts at full resolution. You can also choose to preserve photo details such as EXIF metadata.
